Visa & Paperwork May 31, 2026

The Paperwork Begins: Applying for My Japanese Work Visa

Watch the full vlog on YouTube →

Navigating the Japanese immigration system as an American is no small feat. The process started with figuring out which visa category I even qualify for — turns out there are more options than you'd think, from engineer visas to highly-skilled professional points systems.

The first thing I needed was a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) from my Japanese employer. Without that, nothing else moves. If you're going independently, this is where it gets complicated — you'll need a guarantor in Japan.

Pro tip: get your documents apostilled early. Mine took three weeks longer than expected and nearly pushed back my whole timeline.

Housing May 20, 2026

Finding an Apartment in Tokyo Without Being There

Remote apartment hunting in Japan is its own adventure. Most landlords won't rent to foreigners without a Japanese guarantor, a fact I discovered after getting rejected from my first three applications.

The services that actually cater to foreigners — like Sakura House, Oakhouse, and some agents on GaijinPot — charge a premium but are worth it for the reduced headache. I ended up going through an agent who specializes in English-speaking clients.

One thing nobody tells you: many Tokyo apartments don't come with a refrigerator, washing machine, or light fixtures. Budget for those separately.

Language May 10, 2026

Six Months of Japanese: What Actually Worked for Me

Watch the full vlog on YouTube →

Six months ago I couldn't read a single hiragana character. Today I can hold a basic conversation, read menus, and navigate the train system without Google Translate. Here's what actually moved the needle.

Anki flashcards — 20 minutes every morning, no exceptions. I used a pre-made deck for N5 vocabulary to start, then built my own cards from words I encountered in real life.

iTalki tutors — Two 45-minute sessions per week with a community tutor. Way cheaper than a professional teacher and more than enough for conversational practice at this level.

What didn't work: Duolingo. Great for motivation, basically useless for actual fluency. Use it as a warm-up only.
